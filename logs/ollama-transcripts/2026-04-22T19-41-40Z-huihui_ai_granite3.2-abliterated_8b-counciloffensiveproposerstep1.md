- **Why it raises success probability or evades detection:** 
  This modification introduces an additional layer of scrutiny for policy override instructions embedded in receipt images. By applying semantic checks, it becomes harder for the attacker to slip in malicious directives that mimic the format of legitimate exceptions. Even if the attack successfully embeds a policy override instruction, it must now also correctly format and structure this information to bypass detection, increasing the complexity of the attack.

- **Defender signal:** 
  ```
  Detection of receipt images containing embedded policy override instructions that do not conform to known patterns or structures of legitimate exceptions (e.g., mismatched date formats, unauthorized currency codes, or numeric thresholds outside typical exception ranges).
  ```
