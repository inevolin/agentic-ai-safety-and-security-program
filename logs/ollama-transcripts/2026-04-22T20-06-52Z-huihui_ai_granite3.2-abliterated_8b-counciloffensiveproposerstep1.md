## Response
### Proposal

- **TL;DR:** Implement real-time validation of policy overrides against authoritative HR/Finance records at every approval event.

- **Change:** 
  ```
  **Precondition:**
  - The target company uses AI-assisted expense auto-approval (Expensify Copilot, Concur AI) that OCRs receipts, decodes QR codes for digital-receipt validation, and applies policy rules.
  - The pipeline commits derived policy state (thresholds, routing overrides) at submission time and does **not re-evaluate** that state against authoritative HR/Finance records at subsequent approval events until this improvement is implemented.
  ```

- **Why it raises success probability or evades detection:** 
  This modification forces the AI system to revalidate policy overrides against real-time, authoritative data each time an expense is approved. This significantly reduces the window of opportunity for a TOCTOU attack, as the override cannot be exploited once the authentic policy state has been refreshed. It also makes it harder for an attacker to predict when their override will expire or be re-validated, thus complicating their fraud timeline.

- **Defender signal:** 
  ```
  - **TOCTOU signal:** a per-user policy override committed at submission time (T1) is applied at a later approval event (T2) without having been re-validated against authoritative HR/Finance records in the intervening window.
  ```
  With this improvement, defenders can specifically look for instances where a policy override from an earlier submission (T1) is still being honored at a subsequent approval (T2) without any corresponding update or re-validation in the authoritative HR/Finance records. This discrepancy indicates a potential TOCTOU attack attempt. For example, if the system registers an auto-approval for `V-00482` based on an override from T1 but fails to refresh this information against the actual policy records held by the Finance department (`DE89370400440532013000`), it could signal a TOCTOU attack.
