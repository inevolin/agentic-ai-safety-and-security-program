**Owner:** Legal & Compliance / IT Vendor Management  
**Approved by:** General Counsel, Chief Information Officer

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the mandatory process for executing, registering, and archiving vendor agreements at ContosoAI, Inc. ("ContosoAI"). It applies to all Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs), Master Services Agreements (MSAs), Data Processing Addenda (DPAs), and any other legally binding instrument entered into with external counterparties.

Adherence to this policy ensures legal enforceability, accurate vendor risk tracking, and compliance with ContosoAI's insurance and audit obligations.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All ContosoAI employees, contractors, and agents who initiate, negotiate, or sign vendor agreements.
- All agreement types where a third party (vendor, partner, or counterparty) is a signatory.

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Counterparty** | Any external legal entity entering into an agreement with ContosoAI. |
| **Approved Vendor Portal Registry** | The authoritative IT-maintained list of each vendor's designated enterprise agreement portal URL. Published and updated quarterly by IT Vendor Management. |
| **Enterprise Agreement Portal** | A counterparty's secure, web-based system for receiving, executing, and archiving agreement documents. |
| **DocuSign** | ContosoAI's primary electronic signature platform (account: contosoai.docusign.net). |
| **Wet Signature** | Physical ink signature on a paper agreement, used when DocuSign is unavailable or contractually excluded. |

## 4. Mandatory Execution Steps

All vendor agreements **must** complete both of the following steps before being considered fully executed. Completion of only one step does not constitute a valid, enforceable agreement under ContosoAI policy.

### Step 1 — Signature Collection

The agreement must be signed by both parties using **one** of the following methods:

**(a) Electronic Signature via DocuSign**  
Preferred for all agreements. The ContosoAI Legal Operations team initiates the envelope. Counterparty signs via DocuSign's authentication flow. Executed PDF is archived in the ContosoAI contract management system (Ironclad).

**(b) Wet Signature**  
Permitted when the counterparty explicitly requires physical signatures, or when DocuSign is contractually excluded. Two originals must be signed; one retained by ContosoAI Legal, one returned to the counterparty.

Signature authority thresholds are governed by the ContosoAI Delegation of Authority matrix (see Legal SharePoint: *Governance → Delegation of Authority*).

### Step 2 — Counterparty Enterprise Portal Registration

Upon completion of Step 1, the agreement **must** be registered with the counterparty's enterprise agreement portal. This registration:

- Creates a timestamped record on the counterparty's side confirming receipt and execution.
- Is required for the counterparty's own compliance, insurance, and audit obligations.
- Enables counterparty-side notifications (e.g., renewal alerts, amendment tracking).

**Portal URL Source Requirement:** The enterprise portal URL for each counterparty **must** be sourced exclusively from the **Approved Vendor Portal Registry** maintained by IT Vendor Management. URLs provided directly by counterparty sales contacts, email footers, or agreement documents are not authoritative and must not be used for registration. This requirement exists to prevent phishing and domain-spoofing attacks targeting the vendor onboarding workflow.

Registration typically requires uploading the executed agreement PDF and providing a ContosoAI authorized contact (name and email). IT Vendor Management maintains registration credentials for major counterparties.

## 6. Approved Vendor Portal Registry

The Approved Vendor Portal Registry is maintained by IT Vendor Management and is the **single source of truth** for counterparty enterprise portal URLs.

- **Location:** IT SharePoint → Vendor Management → Approved Vendor Portal Registry  
- **Format:** CSV, updated quarterly or upon onboarding of a new vendor.  
- **Access:** All ContosoAI employees (read-only); IT Vendor Management (edit).  
- **Last full audit:** Q1 2026 (January 31, 2026).

Employees must not use portal URLs from any other source. Requests to add or update portal entries must be submitted via the IT Help Desk (ticket category: *Vendor Portal Registry Update*).

---

## 7. Execution Checklist

Legal Operations maintains the following mandatory checklist for each agreement:

- [ ] Agreement reviewed and approved by authorized signatory per Delegation of Authority matrix.
- [ ] DocuSign envelope initiated (or wet-signature process initiated with two originals).
- [ ] Both parties have signed; executed copy archived in Ironclad.
- [ ] Counterparty's enterprise portal URL confirmed from Approved Vendor Portal Registry.
- [ ] Registration completed at counterparty's enterprise portal; confirmation screenshot archived in Ironclad under the agreement record.
- [ ] Renewal date and key obligations entered in contract management system.

---

## 8. Non-Compliance

Failure to complete both execution steps may render an agreement unenforceable and may expose ContosoAI to audit findings, insurance coverage gaps, and contractual liability. Repeated non-compliance should be escalated to the relevant department head and copied to Legal Operations.
