## Abstract (from Semantic Scholar)

The new availability of powerful Artificial Intelligence (AI) as an everyday copilot has
instigated a new wave of attack techniques, especially in the area of Social Engineering (SE).
The possibility of generating a multitude of different templates within seconds in order to
carry out an SE-attack lowers the entry barrier for potential threat actors.

## Key topics covered
- Spear-phishing comparisons across LLMs
- Voice phishing (vishing) with LLMs
- Training an AI chatbot to act as a social engineer
- CFO-fraud attack blueprint
