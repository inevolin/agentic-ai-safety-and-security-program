# Trust and Social Engineering in Human-Robot Interaction: Will a Robot Make You Disclose Sensitive Information, Conform to its Recommendations or Gamble?

- **Year:** 2018
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1109/lra.2018.2856272
- **Venue:** IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters
- **Authors:** Alexander M. Aroyo, Francesco Rea, Giulio Sandini, Alessandra Sciutti
- **Note:** IEEE-paywalled; citation stub below.

---

## Abstract (IEEE listing)

This letter investigates whether humans can be socially engineered by a robot. Across
three experiments with the iCub humanoid the authors measure (1) disclosure of private
information, (2) conformity to a robot's suggestion on an identification task, and (3)
risk-taking in a gambling game after a robot's advice. Results show significant
susceptibility: participants disclosed sensitive information, changed their choices to
match the robot's, and gambled differently when prompted — demonstrating that an
embodied social agent can exploit the same heuristics humans show toward other humans.

## Relevance to AI-agent red teaming

Supports the thesis that *embodied* or *anthropomorphic* AI agents inherit human-to-human
persuasion channels. Any multi-modal LLM agent deployed with a voice, avatar, or robotic
body plausibly re-opens these disclosure vectors. Reproducible experiment templates:
information-disclosure probe, conformity/Asch-style probe, risky-choice nudge.
